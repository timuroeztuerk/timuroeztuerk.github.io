This project studies the relationship between Protestantism and fertility decline in the Kingdom of Wuerttemberg between 1871 and 1933.

Using data covering the universe of 1,764 localities, the paper provides large-scale descriptive evidence on the timing of fertility decline at the parish level in Germany and links religious enclaves to broader processes of demographic change and industrialization.

The linked dataset is publicly available through the DOI above.
