This paper revisits the long-run political consequences of denominational divisions in Germany.

It traces how Catholic-Protestant cleavages shaped electoral fragmentation on the center-right and influenced the rise of the extreme right, from the NSDAP's electoral breakthrough in the early 1930s to the NPD's emergence in the late 1960s.

The linked repository contains the dataset and supporting research materials.
